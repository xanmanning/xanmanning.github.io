Why I left architecture

Aug 27, 2018

School kids and university students are receiving results, some will be proud and others will be disappointed. There’s plenty of stories online of individuals not getting the results they want and doing well in life, but there is actually another perspective; doing well at your exams and circumstances making life difficult.

Here’s my story from the latter perspective.

TL;DR - A degree in architecture can open more doors than you think, as a creative and logical individual you can re-engineer your career path into many other professions.

On track, about to be derailed.

I left university back in 2012, some 6 years ago now, ready to join the workforce and make a living. Like many before me I had a sense of acomplishment from handing in my final assginments, and dread from the vast quantity of student debt.

At the time of my departure from academia, the UK was just at the tail end of the Great Recession. Jobs in the construction industry where few and far between and such jobs were competative. Being fresh out of Architecture school with 3 degrees (1 Bachelors degree, 2 Masters degrees) and little industry experience I found myself in the awkward position of not being up to scratch for an Architectural Assistant position, but dismissed from jobs in retail.

I went from interview to interview with architecture firms across the Midlands, turned down and left disappointed. I got little response from retail jobs. In my downtime during jobseeking I decided to try and think a bit more laterally, to take some of my peripheral skills from academia and come up with more solutions for finding income.

Turning things around

I reinstalled Linux on my deskop (having previously dabbled before university), I built a MySQL database of architecture firms on a Raspberry Pi. I automated sending CVs to firms using Python on a schedule to try and fulfil my jobseekers requirements, freeing up time for learning more skills.
